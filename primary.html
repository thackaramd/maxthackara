Primary Workshops
KS1–KS2 (Ages 5–11)

My primary workshops are designed to help children discover the joy of storytelling in a relaxed, supportive environment. Through imaginative prompts and guided discussion, students are encouraged to explore their ideas, build confidence, and see themselves as writers.

Sessions are engaging and accessible for all abilities, with a focus on participation, creativity, and enjoyment rather than pressure or performance.

What to expect

Each workshop is carefully structured but flexible, allowing space for creativity and responsiveness to the group.

Students will:
  • Take part in fun, idea-generating activities
  • Explore characters, settings, and story ideas
  • Share thoughts through discussion (with no pressure to perform)
  • Develop confidence in expressing themselves through writing
  • Leave with a sense of achievement and ownership over their work
Approach

My teaching style is calm, encouraging, and inclusive. I understand that for many children, writing can feel daunting—so sessions are designed to reduce pressure and build confidence step by step.

Activities are adapted to suit different ages, abilities, and needs, ensuring every child can access and enjoy the experience.

Workshop formats

Workshops are delivered on school premises as a free, optional extra-curricular session, typically after school, and last between 60 and 75 minutes.

To ensure a high-quality experience, group sizes are limited to a maximum of 10 pupils.

Sessions can be arranged as a one-off workshop or as a short series (e.g. weekly or termly), depending on what suits the school.

Safeguarding & experience

I am a qualified teacher (QTS) with classroom experience across primary age groups. I hold an Enhanced DBS and have a strong understanding of safeguarding practices, including DSL protocols.

I am experienced in supporting a wide range of individual needs and creating a safe, respectful environment where all students feel comfortable contributing.
