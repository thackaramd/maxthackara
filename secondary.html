Secondary Workshops
KS3–KS4 (Ages 11–16)

My secondary workshops are designed to support students in developing their voice, confidence, and control as writers. Through thoughtful prompts and discussion, students explore how ideas can be shaped into engaging, original pieces of writing.

Each workshop balances creativity with skill development, helping students to experiment with style, structure, and tone in a supportive, low-pressure environment.

What to expect

Workshops are structured to encourage both independence and collaboration, giving students the space to think creatively while developing their technical skills.

Students will:
  • Explore a range of creative prompts and themes
  • Experiment with voice, perspective, and narrative style
  • Develop awareness of structure, tone, and language choices
  • Share ideas through discussion (with no pressure to read aloud)
  • Build confidence in expressing their own viewpoints and creative identity
Approach

My teaching style is calm, respectful, and discussion-led. I aim to create an environment where students feel comfortable taking creative risks without fear of judgement.

Workshops are responsive to each group, allowing students to pursue their own ideas while being gently guided in developing their writing skills.

They can also be adapted to support curriculum links where appropriate, including creative writing elements of GCSE English.

Workshop formats

Workshops are delivered on school premises as a free, optional extra-curricular session, typically after school, and last between 60 and 75 minutes.

To ensure a high-quality experience, group sizes are limited to a maximum of 12 pupils.

They can be arranged as a one-off workshop or as a short series (e.g. weekly or termly), depending on what suits the school.

Safeguarding & experience

I am a qualified teacher (QTS) with primary classroom experience and extensive experience working with secondary-aged students through tutoring and creative writing feedback.

I hold an Enhanced DBS and have a strong understanding of safeguarding practices, including DSL protocols.

I am experienced in supporting a wide range of student needs and creating an inclusive environment where all participants feel respected and able to contribute.
